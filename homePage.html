Mareland

MaReLand is an interdisciplinary project and ethnographic study of land reclamation along the Malaysian littoral. Reclamation is the act of creating new land and artificial islands from the sea. The approach that the state and developers are taking is reminiscent of neo-colonial and neoliberal conceptions of the mare nullius (“nobody’s sea”), an empty space to be transformed by large-scale construction projects.

Reclamation

MaReLand explores the competing discourses of sustainability and heritage held by a variety of actors. Situated at the intersection of urban anthropology, critical heritage studies and marine/coastal research, the project explores environmental activism, critiques of heritage hierarchies, urbanisation at sea, and compensation policies fro yet-to-be-built (is)lands to enhance understanding of the right to the sea.
